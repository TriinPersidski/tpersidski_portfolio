About My Journey

I am a positive and creative person with formal technical education and work experience. I am proficient in time management and highly organised with the modern digital tools. I value ongoing development highly and can't stop learning. I am disrupting my career and become a web developer. One day I work location independent. This year I'll master JavaScript.

  • 2001-2007

    My Humble Beginnings

    I got my first website creation experience in my dad's log house production company. I was in charge of all the images and floor plans that went to this site. In late nineties I studied some coding at highschool but I didn't think much of it. After graduating as a timber engineer in 2005 I got my first full time job in a timber construction company. I started as a sales assistant and worked my way up to project manager in two short years. Besides managing construction projects I collaborated with a web developer and created content to company's website (images, graphics and floor plans).

  • 2008-2012

    My first Blog

    I started blogging before I came to Australia in 2008. I wrote weekly about travelling, backpacking and all the interesting happenings when travelling. I kept writing about 4 years and then felt my life was getting a bit normal and settled. I stopped sharing my stories.

  • 2011-2014

    Residential design

    These years were the most stable years in my work career. In Independent Timber Supplies, Rockingham WA, I was responsible for timber quantity take-offs, detailed design and drawings for residential buildings. I did lots of sewing, felting and ran few craft workshops on my downtime to balance the material estimation and drafting work tasks. The ocean "in my front yard" became a big part of my life.

  • 2014-2016

    Time for change

    I prepared my motherhood with many new projects because I was certain that stay-at-home-mums do nothing all day. It's taken me almost 4 years to find my new career path in web development and learn the new skills. Working from home is something I really enjoy. Kids and household duties add definitely extra challenge to it but managing time and tasks efficiently has given me the confidence I can work remotely.

  • 2017-Current

    Web Development

    One thing leads to another and my interest to web development has increased over the past 2 years. I've learned HTML5, CSS3, JavaScript and use some popular frameworks like Bootstrap and JQuery. Design's been always a part of my life, starting from designing clothes and timber furniture to designing residential houses. I want to focus on grafic design and create beautiful online experiences in near future.
